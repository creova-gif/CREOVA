SEEN by Creova addresses a real cultural gap in Canada's media landscape. Here's the breakdown:

The Problem

Canada is one of the world's most multicultural countries, yet mainstream streaming platforms (Netflix, Spotify, YouTube) are dominated by American and globally homogenized content. This means:

Indigenous, Black Canadian, francophone, immigrant, and other equity-deserving communities are chronically underrepresented in the stories Canadians can access.
Cultural knowledge — oral histories, community narratives, multilingual stories — gets lost or overlooked in algorithm-driven platforms optimized for mass engagement.
Canadian creators from these communities have no dedicated, safe space to publish and own their work, and often lose IP rights to larger platforms.
The Solution SEEN Provides

SEEN is a culturally-centred storytelling platform — think a cinematic, audio-first story world experience — built specifically around Canadian identity and underrepresented voices:

Story worlds (not just content) that combine narration, ambient audio, and text — covering topics like Montreal's jazz scene, Black Atlantic Canada, Indigenous languages, immigrant first-generation stories, and more, in English, French, and Spanish.
Creator ownership first — creators keep full IP rights; the platform makes no claims on their work.
Culturally-sensitive moderation — content flagging that includes "cultural appropriation" as a category, with escalation paths to cultural advisors (not just automated bans).
CMF (Canada Media Fund) compliance — the platform is designed to qualify for federal Canadian cultural funding, tracking metrics like Canadian content hours, equity-deserving creator demographics, and multilingual content by province/territory.
Accessible by design — multilingual support and accessibility customization built into onboarding.
In short: SEEN is Canada's answer to the cultural representation gap in streaming — a platform where underrepresented Canadian storytellers publish, own, and share their work with audiences who want authentic cultural narratives, backed by federal funding infrastructure (CMF).
